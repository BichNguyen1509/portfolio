Origin story

A friend learning Mandarin at a private center couldn't use language learning apps – it had its own vocabulary, not his class's. So he didn't review at all. That gap between your classroom content and structured daily review is what LangLearn solves.

Problem & Solution
❌ Problem

Language learning apps (e.g. Duolingo) use their own standard library. Learners enrolled in private centers can't practice their class vocabulary with any existing app.

✅ Solution

Photograph your lesson pages → Bot OCRs it → Flashcards generated instantly → SM-2 schedules the next review → Reminders sent automatically.

Ecommerce-Native Brand Analysis
📄 Description
An analysis of two top-performing DTC food brands on TikTok Shop — exploring product architecture, pricing strategy, and communication to understand where their growth comes from.
🛠️ Tools
Excel Google Slides
🏷️ Type
Ecommerce Market Research
An analysis of two top-performing TikTok Shop food brands — studying how they structure product, pricing, bundles, and content to build outsized growth.
Context

The snacks & instant food market in Vietnam has seen a strong rise of TikTok Shop-native players with outsized livestream & affiliate spend. This analysis looks at two top performers — Mì Cay Ba Báo and Ăn Cùng Bà Tuyết — to understand how they structure product, bundle, price and communication.

Data & Methodology

Sources: Shopee & TikTok Shop official stores — product listings, pricing, bundle structure, and sales volume collected manually.

What I collected: For each brand — listing name, SKU variants, original price, discounted price, promotion rate, units sold, bundle type, product lines included, GWP (gift-with-purchase), and channel tags in listing name.

How I processed it:

Mì Cay Ba Báo
  • Classified listings by bundle type and calculated % units sold by bundle type
  • Extracted 30D quantity per listing → mapped back to raw SKUs
  • Parsed product lines per listing → calculated cross-line add-on frequency
  • Parsed channel tags from listing names → classified listings by sales channel
  • Built price range ladders, mapped min/max pricing and promotion strategy for GWP
  • Calculated price per pack and estimated revenue per listing
Ăn Cùng Bà Tuyết
  • Classified SKU variants by product line (chân gà, đùi gà, snack ướt,...)
  • Classified each SKU into a bundle type (Single/Same-line/Cross-line)
  • Parsed product line combinations per listing to count cross-line mix frequency
  • Built price range ladders and mapped min/max pricing by bundle type
  • Calculated promotion range (min/max discount %) per bundle type
  • Calculated GWP rate to show % SKUs with attached gift items
Brand 1 — Mì Cay Ba Báo

One-product deep, multi-layer bundle

Mì Cay Ba Báo is heavily concentrated - just a single product (instant noodles - 1 hero SKU). All 46 listings serve a single purpose: scale bundle size from 1 -> 3 -> 6 -> 10 -> 20 -> 30 packs. The 10-pack is the clear hero, driving the bulk of unit volume and cross-line add-ons.

The cross-line add-ons strategy is equally systematic. Of all upgrade/add-on listings, 80% link to ớt, 30% to kim chi, 25% to muối chấm. These aren't random assortments - they create a 'complete meal kit' perception and pull AOV higher without requiring new product development.

Promotion is strictly bundle-led. Cross-line bundles (76.3%) while single SKUs carry almost no promotion (5.1%). The brand is intentional: brand pushes transparent bulk sizing to best-selling bundles and uses deep discounts only on LIVE / FLASH SALE to compel fast buyers.

Key content: Raw operational footage

Brand channel (@Ăn Vặt Ba Báo: 372.9k followers on TikTok 2024) — mostly unscripted footage from the warehouse, packing lines, and franchise views. Facebook (84k followers) repurposes TikTok content and pushes impulse deals via Messenger.

The content strategy is trust-first. Show the operation at scale (warehouse footage, order volume) to build trust before pushing commerce. Livestream is used to create urgency, not to discount — cross-line bundles (10x) only 2.5–12.5% even during live sessions.

Key observations — Mì Cay Ba Báo

  • Depth over breadth: 1 SKU = 46 listings outperforms wide portfolios by maximizing a single product's potential
  • Livestream = urgency mechanism, not a massive discount channel
  • Operational transparency (warehouse, packing, franchise) as a trust factor that punches above polished content for mass market buyers
Brand 2 — Ăn Cùng Bà Tuyết

Far wider portfolio — 9 product lines, 136 SKU variants

Where Mì Cay Ba Báo goes deep on one product, Bà Tuyết goes wide — 9 lines across chân gà, đùi gà, snack ướt, bim bim, thịt sấy, etc. 85% of listings are bundles, and ~50% push cross-line mixes spanning multiple product categories in a single order.

Bundle naming is a brand asset in itself. Names like 'Combo Tứ Đại', 'Combo Nhất Thống', 'Combo Tê Cay', 'Cốt Điện' borrow from gaming and kiếm hiệp slang — creating a brand universe that Gen Z buyers relate to and remember. GWP (gift-with-purchase) appears across 24% of SKU variants, used as a conversion nudge rather than a straight discount.

A character-driven universe

With 3.2M followers on TikTok (the primary channel — 100% original content), all audio revolves around 'Bà Tuyết' — not just as a mascot, but as the central storyteller, community anchor, and face of transparency and CSR/charity mechanics. Facebook (238k followers) acts as a traffic driver for commerce push. This mirrors the Ba Báo approach at a larger scale and with a more developed brand identity.

Key observations — Ăn Cùng Bà Tuyết

  • A single consistent character replaces the need for KOL spend and creates stronger brand recall
  • Bundle naming as entertainment extends the brand universe beyond pure product description
  • GWP used as conversion nudge, not margin erosion — discount rates are carefully managed under 40% on their lowest bracket
